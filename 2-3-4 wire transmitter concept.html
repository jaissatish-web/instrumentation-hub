Fundamentals

What Exactly Is a Transmitter?

It's the translator between the physical world and your control system

A transmitter takes a physical measurement (like pressure or temperature) and turns it into a 4-20 mA electrical signal that your PLC can understand.

Core Concept

The 4-20 mA Loop — Live!

Watch the current flow and see how the value changes

💡 Why 4 mA and not 0 mA?

4 mA is "live zero" — if the wire breaks, the current drops to 0 mA, and the PLC knows there's a fault. Smart!

🔧 Technician Tip

Always measure current in series (not voltage). A multimeter in mA mode placed in the loop tells you the exact process value.

2-Wire Systems

The 2-Wire Transmitter

Power and signal travel together on the same two wires — simple and clever

⚡ How It Works

  • 🔌The 24V power supply sends voltage into the loop
  • 📡The transmitter draws 4-20 mA from the loop to power itself
  • 📊The current value is the measurement — e.g., 12 mA = 50%
  • 🖥️The PLC reads this current through a 250Ω shunt resistor
🧑‍🔧 Field Tip

If you disconnect any wire, the loop opens and the PLC reads 0 mA — that's how it detects a broken wire. Always check continuity first!

2-Wire: Pros & Cons

When to use it — and when to pick something else

✅ Advantages

  • 💰Lowest wiring cost — only 2 conductors needed
  • 🔧Simple installation — fewer terminals to mess up
  • 🛡️Intrinsic safety — low power = safer in hazardous areas
  • 📏Long cable runs — current signal is noise-immune
  • 🔋Self-powered — no separate power feed needed

⚠️ Limitations

  • ⚡Low power budget — can't drive displays or heaters
  • 🌡️Limited for RTDs — 3-wire RTDs need more conductors
  • 📊No local display — power budget is too tight
  • 🔌Voltage drop — long runs may starve the device
🏭 Common Uses: Pressure, level, simple temperature — the workhorses of industry.
3-Wire Systems

The 3-Wire Transmitter

Separate power supply with a shared ground — more power, more flexibility

🔧 How It Works

  • 🔴Wire 1 (+V): Power supply positive to transmitter
  • ⚫Wire 2 (GND): Common ground shared by power and signal
  • 🔵Wire 3 (Signal): 4-20 mA output to PLC
  • 💡More power for local displays and advanced features
✅ When to Choose 3-Wire

Flow meters with local displays, devices that need more power than a 2-wire loop provides, or when you want separate power without full isolation.

4-Wire Systems

The 4-Wire Transmitter

Fully isolated — power and signal are completely separate for maximum accuracy

🔧 How It Works

  • 🔴Wire 1 & 2: Dedicated power supply (+V and -V)
  • 🔵Wire 3 & 4: Dedicated signal output (+Sig and -Sig)
  • 🛡️Galvanic isolation between power and signal — no ground loops
  • ⚡Highest power for complex electronics and displays
🛡️ Isolation Barrier
POWER SIDE ⛔ ISOLATION ⛔ SIGNAL SIDE

The isolation barrier blocks ground loops and protects sensitive signal circuits from power-side noise and spikes.

4-Wire: Benefits & Applications

When full isolation and high power are non-negotiable

✅ Advantages

  • 🛡️Complete galvanic isolation — zero ground loops
  • ⚡Highest power budget — drives displays, heaters, relays
  • 📊Best accuracy — no power supply noise on signal
  • 🔌Flexible power — can use AC or different DC voltages
  • 🌐Multi-output — some provide 4-20mA + digital

🏭 Common Applications

  • 🧪Gas analyzers — complex electronics need isolation
  • ⚗️pH / conductivity — sensitive electrochemical measurements
  • 🔥High-temp furnaces — heated sensor heads need extra power
  • 📡Smart transmitters — HART + display + diagnostics
  • 🏥Critical safety loops — SIL-rated applications
💡 Remember: 4-wire costs more in cabling but saves money by preventing ground loop issues and signal corruption in critical measurements.

Watch Out

Common Installation Mistakes

Avoid these errors — they cost hours of troubleshooting

🔌

Wrong Polarity

Swapping + and − on a 2-wire transmitter gives 0 mA. The PLC shows "Open Loop." Always check terminal labels!

🔄

Ground Loops

Connecting signal ground to earth at multiple points creates circulating currents. Ground at ONE point only.

🎯

Wrong AI Terminals

Connecting a 4-20 mA signal to a voltage input (0-10V) channel gives wrong readings. Verify your AI card type.

✂️

Open Loop

A loose terminal or broken wire breaks the loop. PLC reads 0 mA. Check continuity with a multimeter.

🛡️

Broken Shield

Not grounding the cable shield (or grounding both ends) lets noise corrupt your signal. Ground shield at PLC end only.

⚡

Overvoltage

Applying 120V AC to a 24V DC transmitter destroys it instantly. Always verify voltage before connecting.

Safety First

Safety Best Practices

Non-negotiable rules for every field technician

🔒 Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)
Always de-energize and lock out before working
⚡ Verify Voltage
Use a multimeter to confirm 0V before touching terminals
📋 Check Datasheet
Verify wire colors, terminal assignments, and voltage ratings
🔍 Confirm Terminals
Double-check you're on the right AI channel and terminal block
🔥 Hazardous Area Precautions
In Zone 0/1/2 areas, use IS barriers and certified equipment
🧤 Use Proper PPE
Insulated gloves, safety glasses, and arc-rated clothing
📸 Take Photos Before
Document existing wiring before making changes
🧪 Test Before Full Power
Use a loop calibrator to verify signal before energizing the PLC
